✨ WORD PUNS ✨

Get ready to master the art of wordplay in advertising!

What is a PUN? 🤔

A joke exploiting the different possible meanings of a word or the fact that there are words which sound alike but have different meanings.

🎯 Your Mission:

  • Look at the advertisement.
  • Discuss with your classmates: What is the hidden meaning?
  • Click "Reveal" to see if you are right!

Mission Starts! 🚀

Let's begin with something familiar.

Level 1: Cantonese Puns 🇭🇰

Local Slang & Homophones

Get ready to decode these local ads!

Level 1: Cantonese Pun

IKEA Ice Cream 🍦

🔍 Click image to enlarge

Literal Meaning: "Eat my tofu-flavored ice cream."

The Pun (Slang): In Cantonese, "eating someone's tofu" (食豆腐) is slang for taking advantage of someone (flirting/teasing)! 😏

Level 1: Cantonese Pun

Cafe de Coral 🍛

🔍 Click image to enlarge

Original Idiom: 唯我獨尊 (Only I am supreme / I am the best).

The Pun: "Supreme" (獨尊) sounds exactly like "Baked Pork Chop" (焗豬) in Cantonese! 🐷🔥

Level 1: Cantonese Pun (Trend-jacking)

IKEA Mattress 🛏️

🔍 Click image to enlarge

The Catchphrase: 破・地褥 (Po Dei Juk)

The Pun: "Mattress" (褥) and "Hell" (獄) are exact homophones in Cantonese! Both are pronounced juk6.

IKEA brilliantly hijacked the title of the hit HK movie "破·地獄" (The Last Dance) to sell their mattresses. A perfect example of trend-jacking! 🎬🛏️

Level 1 Cleared! 🎉

You survived the Cantonese wordplay.

Level 2: Bilingual Pun (Chinglish)

McDonald's Rice Burger 🍔

🔍 Click image to enlarge

English Word: Fantastic (Amazing).

The Pun: "Fan" (飯) means RICE in Cantonese. Combined together, it becomes a Fan-tastic rice burger! 🍚✨

Level 2: Bilingual Pun (Chinglish)

Bakery Egg Tarts 🥧

🔍 Click image to enlarge

Original Phrase: So Exciting!

The Pun: "So" becomes "Sou" (酥 - flaky pastry). "Exciting" becomes "Egg-citing". Perfect for flaky egg tarts! 🥚🤯

Level 3: English Puns

Homophones & Double Meanings 🤯

Let's see how well you know your English vocabulary!

Level 3: English (Homophones)

Train Station Ad 🚆

🔍 Click image to enlarge

Mechanism: Homophones (Words that sound exactly the same).

The Pun: Buy (purchase) and Bye (goodbye). If you buy a ticket, you can say bye to the penalty fine! 👋🎫

Level 3: English (Double Meaning)

Train Station Ad Part 2 🚆

🔍 Click image to enlarge

Mechanism: One word, two entirely different meanings.

Meaning 1: Okay, suit yourself. 🤷‍♂️
Meaning 2: A financial penalty. 💸

"You don't want to pay for a ticket? Okay (Fine), then pay the penalty (Fine)."

Level 3: English (Sound-Alike)

Coffee Mug ☕

🔍 Click image to enlarge

Original Phrase: Express yourself. 🗣️

The Pun: "Express" sounds very similar to "Espresso" (strong coffee). Perfect for a morning coffee mug! ☕✨
